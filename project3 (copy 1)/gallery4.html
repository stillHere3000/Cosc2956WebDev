Hermitage Museum

The Hermitage Museum is the second largest art museum in the world, with over 3 million items in its collection. It was founded in 1764 by Catherine the Great, and has been open to the public since 1852. The museum is housed in the Winter Palace, which was the official residence of the Russian monarchs from 1732 to 1917. The museum's collection includes works by Leonardo da Vinci, Michelangelo, Raphael, Titian, Rembrandt, Rubens, and many others.
